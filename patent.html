Oppurtunity/Patent History

In 2001, Gus invented the Super CMOS(SCMOS(TM)) device Technology, using a newly invented material CLtSBD(TM) to make embedded Schottky Barrier Diodes within the CMOS FET diffusion beds, sharing the source/drain terminals. By using the smallest switching element in the world, new DTL OR tree and AND tree can be designed with the most compacted layouts. and many memory cells can be designed. These newly structured memory cells may form highly efficient Logic gates, and DRAM, SRAM, and Non Volatile PROM cells reusable arrays for data processing and storage functions. with the properties of Highest Speed, Lowest Power dissipation, and least chip Areas, together with Yield, and Reliability (SPAYR) benefits. Gus' work was presented to TSMC, SMIC, Charter, MaLab, AMD, Intel, HuaWei, Synopsis, MSFT, Xlinx, and SLSI, but nobody wanted to develop the technology because of the unknown costs and risks.

In April 2013, Gus co-funded the SLSI with Mr. Ven Lee, who was the Ex VP of Engineering of LSI logic. Gus was the CTO and Ven is the CEO, Gus assigned his SCMOS Patent grants to the company, and Ven is responsible in marketing and funding. SLSI spent 44 months w/o funding but in 2017, attracted $2.6M to develop test chips. In Summer SCMOS(TM) won 3 Big innovation awards in Santa Clara (First Place), Boston (First Place), and ChangChun, JiLin, China (Second Place), But we did not win VC funding from any Science Park VC.

By August 2017, SLSI did not deliver a working test chip, then Gus was discharged by the CEO without valid reasons. by the next August 20, 2018, Gus was voted out from the 3 men board and all stock holders. He was denied to access nor use the SCMOS IP and all board records. By November, 2019, Gus formed ASST to pursue SCMOS2 patent filing and business development.

US semiconductor stocks reached peak in 20 years in 2017. Then China and the US trade war broke out in 2018, and Pandemics broke out in January. Gus and Ven had litigation over SLSI disputes unresolved. Ven offered rehiring back but we could not reach settlement to the terms.

Now that US is determined to rebuild the new IC manufacturing lines and IT assembly plants to lead the world again with TSMC engineers. We can work together to implement our SCMOS processes and circuits for the AI chips and modules of the G5 and G6 devices with much lower facility and labor costs. With our milestone plan for the 130 nm manufacturing line, networks can be much reduced and with better density and use 2D CMOS FET and the embedded LtSBD(TM) circuits may offer much higher speed operations than the CMOS 2D FET circuits.

Once we proved our concept, then we can implement the 28 nm and the 20 nm node SCMOS devices. We can grow $T revenue with the 2D process lines to take over the IOE marketing.
